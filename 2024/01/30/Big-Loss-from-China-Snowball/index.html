Big Loss from China Snowball

As underperforming in the China and Hong Kong stock market performance, Chinese investors hit by the big loss in the ‚ÄòSnowball‚Äô derivatives. What is snowball ? How does it work ?

Snowball is structural derivatives that tie to the performance of an underlying index. It offers coupons as long as that index stay within a predetermined range. This product normally used in the China market, and it ties with CSI 500 or CSI 1000. (CSI 500 and CSI 1000 has higher volatility than CSI 300)

How does the Snowball work?

If index rise above the top of the range, then it will trigger ‚Äúknock-out‚Äù, which the contract will terminates and investor can receives the coupons for the period of that date. If the index drops below the bottom of the range, then it trigger ‚Äúknock-in‚Äù, in which the holders receive no coupon and potentially lose part of the their capital.

Let‚Äôs jump into see the details of how to make profit or loss in the snowball :

  • Gain: during the contract time, if the index price above the top range, the contract will be terminated immediately, investors would receive the coupons based on the period

  • Gain: if the index price within the range throughout the whole period, then investors would receive the whole coupons at the expired date.

  • No Gain / Loss: if the index price fall below the button range price for once, but the price at the expired date is within range, then the investor gain/loss=0.

  • Loss: if the index price fall below the button range price at the expire date, investor loss=initiate price - ending price

How does the broker gain from the snowball ?

Selling one snowball product means buy a put option. Broker would use Delta 0 strategy.

In the long put option, the delta would be small as the underlying asset have higher price, so the number of underlying asset used for hedging would be smaller. But if the underlying asset have lower price (put option have higher price), the delta would be bigger (look at the slope), then broker need more underlying asset for hedge. In this case, the broker would buy more asset if the underlying asset price is low, and sell high when the underlying asset price is high ‚Äî> „ÄêÈ´òÊäõ‰Ωé‰π∞„Äë.

for the these broker, they will gain profit within this dynamic market. If the underlying asset price increase, their profit = underlying asset gain - investor gain. But if the underlying asset price decrease, the investor would take all the risk as brokers have no risk about it.

Normally, ‚ÄúKnock-in‚Äù price would be the 80-70% of initial price, and ‚Äúknow-out‚Äù price would be the 103% of initial price. Why the percentage is not the same ?

Why the knock-out percentage is too small ?

To broker, selling the snowball product equivalent buy the put option the may point is that if the ‚Äúknock-out‚Äù price is too high, it‚Äôs more likely that the investor would stay within the range and broker would need to pay more amount of coupon to the investor. It would not be benefit the broker to gain profit.

Why knock-in percentage is too high ?

When the underlying asset price that broker hold is decreasing, the broker actually gain loss. But since the broker sell the snowball product (equivalent to buy the put option), the loss would be offset. Also the high knock in percentage can give the investor sense of security, this can attract investor to invest.

Knock-in and knock-out observation time

  • Knock-in observation time would be everyday: so it means that the broker would observe the price of the underlying asset that check whether the price is below the range.

  • Knock-out observation time would be once a month : so it means that the broker to count the knock-out only once a month

Since the knock-out event is loss for the broker, so broker would prefer decrease the observation frequency. As the broker can gain profit from the ‚Äúknock-in ‚Äúevent, so the broker definitely trace the ‚Äúknock-in‚Äù event more frequently. Since the probability of ‚Äúknock-in‚Äù is relatively small since the percentage is high, so it‚Äôs relatively fair that broker do in this way.

Profit from Backwardation

For the snowball product, the underlying asset rely on is CSI 500 and CSI 1000, but broker would use the CSI 500/ CSI 1000 commodity to do hedging. In China market, it contains price differences between commodity and the actual asset price especially in the CSI 500 and CSI 1000. In this case the broker can earn much profit from it.

For example, currently the CSI 1000 initial price is 1000, CSI 1000 commodity price is 800. If trigger the ‚ÄòKnock-out‚Äô event at the expiration date as CSI 1000 price at 1100, then the broker can get the underlying asset from the commodity expire date, gain = 1100-800=300, plus some of leverage, then broker can still have more profit after paying the coupon to investor

If trigger the ‚ÄòKnock-in‚Äô event at the expiration date as CSI 1000 price at 700. In this situation, the broker loss = 800-700=100 , the investor loss = 1000-700=300, the loss from investor can cover the loss from broker, the broker still can earn 200 in this case.

Overall, snowball product contains high risk for the investor, since there is no loss protection if underlying asset price decrease, while it contains profit limit as the underlying asset price increase. But the broker only will get loss when there is no too much fluctuation in price.
